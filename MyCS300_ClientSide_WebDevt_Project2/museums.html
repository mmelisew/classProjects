Museums

Museum of Science and Industry

The Museum of Science and Industry is located in Hyde Park on Lakeshore Drive. An amazing museum to experience science first-hand. Learning activities for people of all ages that allow you to interact with many of the exhibits.

Field Museum

Located at the Museum Campus, the Field Museum is the perfect place to learn about natural history and ancient cultures. There are multiple activities for kids and adults, as well as 3D movies that will teach you all sorts of interesting lessons. Do not forget to say hi to “Sue,” the world’s largest Tyrannosaurus rex while you are there.

Shedd Aquarium

Located at the Museum Campus, the Shedd Aquarium is the right place to go to if you are looking for an amazing dolphin show with a view of Lake Michigan. Its beautiful specimens are sure to leave you mesmerized with their colors and abilities. From small fish to sharks and penguins, Shedd Aquarium has it all.

Adler Planetarium

If you are just done with Earth, make sure to visit the Adler Planetarium at the Museum Campus. Before you even go in, you will notice the splendid view of the Chicago skyline, so that is a good plus in anyone’s book. However, the real show starts inside with interactive exhibits of the stars, moon and far-away planets, as well as sky shows.

The Art Institute of Chicago

Located downtown on Michigan Avenue with two majestic lions guarding its entrance, the Art Institute of Chicago will surely quench your thirst for art. Historical exhibits of oil paintings, sculptures, medieval armor, old artifacts and much more, you will find yourself lost in this place for hours starting and nothing but gorgeous art.
